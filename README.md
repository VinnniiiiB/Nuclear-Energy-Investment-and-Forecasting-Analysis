# ☢️ UK Energy Demand Forecasting & Nuclear Investment Analysis

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project was developed as part of a consultancy-focused energy investment study for Frazer Nash Consultancy (UK).

The objective was to forecast future UK energy demand and evaluate the long-term investment viability of Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) compared with Large Reactors (LRs).

The project combines forecasting, regression modelling, scenario analysis, risk assessment, and investment evaluation techniques to support data-driven strategic decision-making within the energy sector.

---

## 🔍 Project Objectives

- Forecast UK energy demand from historical trends and macroeconomic indicators.
- Identify key drivers influencing future energy consumption.
- Estimate future nuclear energy requirements under different scenarios.
- Compare investment feasibility between SMRs and Large Reactors.
- Quantify financial and operational risks associated with nuclear deployment.
- Provide strategic recommendations to support long-term investment decisions.

## 💰 Investment Evaluation

The study compared investment strategies for:

### Large Reactors (LR)

vs

### Small Modular Reactors (SMR)

Assessment areas included:

- Capital Expenditure
- Construction Timelines
- Return on Investment
- Scalability
- Operational Efficiency
- Environmental Impact

### Key Findings

- SMRs enable phased investment deployment.
- Shorter construction timelines accelerate returns.
- Learning curve effects reduce future deployment costs.
- Lower capital concentration reduces investment risk.

## 🏆 Final Recommendation

Based on forecasting, investment evaluation, and risk modelling results:

![Cost Benefit Analysis](Cost%20Benefit%20Analysis%20.png)

The final analysis indicates that Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) provide a more scalable and lower-risk investment approach, delivering increasing long-term benefits while reducing capital concentration and operational risk.

### Key Conclusions

- Future UK energy demand remains substantial through 2050.
- Nuclear energy continues to play a critical role in long-term energy security.
- Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) offer greater deployment flexibility.
- FOAK-to-NOAK learning effects significantly improve SMR economics.
- Risk exposure is lower through phased deployment compared with large-scale reactor investments.
- Earlier operationalisation improves long-term investment returns.
